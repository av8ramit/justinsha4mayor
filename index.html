Covid-19

As the search for a vaccine continues, long-term effects of the epidemic grow ever more significant. As we switch to digital, we need experienced tech-savvy leadership who can help reduce the learning curve for all our residents and transform Fremont into a transparent city. As Mayor, I will leverage new and emerging technologies to create permanent information flow for residents. This includes push notifications, data visualizations, and functionalities like contact tracing. We will outline COVID-19 infection rates by zip code and date, and we will provide optional filters for the surrounding cities to make data useful and easily accessible to residents. We will reduce information asymmetry and boost consumer confidence by implementing ordinances requiring high-foot-traffic businesses like Costco to engage in regular employee testing and deep cleaning upon positive employee tests. This helps our recovery and helps residents feel safe. I will also establish a permanent, cost-effective, and free COVID-19 testing station in Fremont, whether drive-through or rapid on-the-spot PCR. We also need to make sure that residents know about and can easily access resources provided by the County. Finally, I will ramp up and champion community outreach, so that our community members can be better equipped to protect themselves, our seniors, and others in our community who are at higher risk.

Smart Growth

Over the past 15 years, Fremont has become overdeveloped with unaffordable condos that put great strains on our schools and roads. We need elected officials who keep their promises and will fight for all residents, including our young people and seniors. I pledge to take a community-centered approach in fulfilling our Regional Housing Need Allocation (RHNA) requirements. We need to take a smart, forward-thinking, and balanced approach in developing more moderate-income housing. Moreover, the associated issue is traffic. We must leverage existing technologies to anticipate and address problems such as traffic gridlock and road congestion that will inevitably return once shelter-in-place is fully lifted. We need leadership that understands technology and has a successful track record in thinking outside the box. As Mayor, I will proactively work to implement non-controversial IoT technology solutions, so that we can build a Smarter City with less traffic congestion, more mixed-use planning, and more recreation. Fremont must become transparent and accountable to community desires and needs.

Fremont For All

I believe in a Family-Friendly Fremont. We must use our budget efficiently, innovate additional private-public partnerships, and invest our city’s finite dollars to deliver best value and address the issues our residents care most about. I believe that raising community awareness about key issues is the first step to fostering a safe and family-friendly city. Public safety and racial equity are not mutually exclusive concepts—in fact, they are intertwined. I would like to help build a Fremont where health professionals also have the opportunity to be first responders to public health calls, so that psychiatric or medical needs can be triaged. We also need to address the homelessness crisis by implementing regional solutions. I will advocate for our state legislators to explore Universal Basic Income (UBI), so that we can support our service workers, young families, and seniors and keep our most vulnerable housed at the state and regional level. Fremont services are currently underutilized due to a combination of poor execution, poor community outreach, and their lack of functionality for residents. We should focus on creating a more thoughtful city by prioritizing walking paths for our residents, creating more socially distanced recreational activities such as tai chi classes at our public parks, and better supporting our local restaurants. Fremont needs leadership who will rise to the challenge of advocating for our community and putting our community first.
